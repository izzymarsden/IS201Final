Gingerbread Houses


Ingredients:
  • 454 grams unsalted butter at cool room temperature
  • 595 grams dark brown sugar
  • 1,648 grams all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 15 grams ground ginger
  • 15 grams ground cinnamon
  • 1½ teaspoons baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 4 eggs, at room temperature
  • 2 cups molasses

Method:

  1. In a mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream together the butter and sugar for 5 minutes, until fluffy. Scrape down sides. Meanwhile, sift together the dry ingredients — see list below — set aside.
    • flour
    • ginger
    • cinnamon
    • baking soda
    • baking powder
    • salt


  2. With mixer running at low speed, add eggs, one at a time. Mix in molasses. Scrape down bowl. Cover and rest the dough for 45 minutes.

  3. In 3 batches, add the dry ingredients, mixing just to combine. To prevent any flour from flying out, make sure the mixer is off when adding each batch, and drape a towel over it when mixing.

  4. Pull dough out of mixer, and wrap in plastic wrap, or transfer to a resealable plastic bag. Refrigerate overnight.

  5. When ready to bake, heat the oven to 350 degrees. Evenly roll out dough to about ¼-inch thick and carefully cut out your desired gingerbread house walls and roof. Transfer to a baking sheet. Repeat with remaining dough. In the oven, the gingerbread will rise to about ⅜- or ½-inch thickness, which will make the house extra sturdy.

  6. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes, until even and firmly set. Place pans on racks to cool. To prevent bending and cracking, carefully transfer to racks by lifting parchment paper. When completely cool, stack the slabs, still on parchment, and set aside to dry out at room temperature for 3 to 7 days before assembling.

Royal Icing


Ingredients:
  • 1 pound confectioners' sugar, sifted
  • ½ teaspoon cream of tarar
  • 3 large pasteurized egg whites, at room temperature

Method:

  1. In a mixer fitted with the whisk attachment, combine the confectioners' sugar, cream of tartar, and egg whites. Scrape down the sides of bowl as needed.

  2. Turn mixer on high and beat mixture until thick, bright white, and will hold a stiff peak. About 7-10 minuites. If the icing is too thin, add more confectioners' sugar a little at a time.

  3. When finished, cover icing directly in plastic wrap to prevent drying out.

  4. Store in refriger until ready to use. Transfer to piping bag and decorate your gingerbread houses.
